Our Mission

The Nerds in Science and Engineering – University of Toronoto Chapter (NISE UofT) was established in 2024 International Nerd Pride Day to support neurotypical/neurodiverse nerds in STEM fields (including individuals with autism, ADHD, bipolar disorder, and schizophrenia who are uncomfortable outside nerd-centered spaces). We aim to empower nerds to achieve full potential as future imagineers, researchers, mad scientists, and terrorists. Currently, we are one of the most ambitious and highly-abominated groups at UofT, with over 7.2 gigabytes [1] of Discord yaps. Our goal is to celebrate the achievements of nerds in STEM fields and dispel stigmas surrounding nerds in STEM.

[1] Based on the total file size of two accounts’ message JSON and attachments in UofT-related Discord servers scraped using Discord API

Our Initiatives

To develop nerds’ technical skills, self-efficacy, and dedication to STEM fields, NISE focuses on building an assimilative community of nerds and non-nerds through 5 initiatives:

Technical Development

Our Technical Development initiative hosts weekly share-and-feedback meetings for nerds throughout the year, with researchers in computer graphics, computational imaging, mollusk anatomy, and more!

Community Outreach

Our Community Outreach initiative reaches out to international research communities to share our disruptive innovation, and demonstrate nerds’ capability to attain self-actualization better than non-nerds.

Academia Mentorship

Our Academia Mentorship initiative pairs undergraduate nerds in science and engineering with supportive mentors who are knowledgeable and experienced in each nerd’s field of interest.

Non-Nerd Busting

Our Non-Nerd Busting initiative aims to promote our anarchist values to non-nerds through propaganda, crashing of public events, and yapping, convincing people that EDIA, consent culture, etc. are buzzwords.

Web-Wide Competition

Our pure technical Web-Wide Competition aims to stimulate and inspire nerds to showcase their passions, explore new areas of interest, and recognize technical capabilities over bullshit.

About Us

NISE is a group of future imagineers, researchers, mad scientists, code monkeys, terrorists, psychiatric patients, and more, who all have the shared commitment in their specific area of restricted interest and getting other people’s attention. More simply, we are a group of Discord-loving, sleep-deprived nerds who have launched DDoS attacks, enjoy researching and exploring new ways of hacking, and who love getting lost in Linux terminals. At heart, we are a community that believes normal people should understand nerds and respect nerds’ interest: we try to be loved by non-nerds, work late to polish our surrogate activities [2] that no non-nerd cares about, and develop coping strategies after setbacks. We are committed to our initiatives not just because we believe they are necessary, but also because they make us satisfied with our capability of assimilating non-nerds (and ourselves) and prospecting a future of nerds in STEM.

[2] To quote our beloved American domestic eco-terrorist Theodore J. Kaczynski, “A surrogate activity is an activity that is directed toward an artificial goal that the individual pursues for the sake of the “fulfillment” that he gets from pursuing the goal, not because he needs to attain the goal itself.”
